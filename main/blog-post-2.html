How Green Onion Chex have Become the Symbol of Democracy in South Korea

In December 2004, South Korea, Kellogg's released a plan for a new product. They made a viral marketing campaign in which people would vote for the next 'president of Chex nation' and the winner will be released as the new product.

Candidate Cheki (chocolate) on the left Candidate Chaka (green onion) on the right

Here are the two candidates. Cheki from MilkChoco Party- "If I'm elected as the president, I will double the amount of cholocate in Chex!". And Chaka from Green Onion Party- "If I'm elected as the president, I will release the new green onion flavored Chex!"

As expected, Cheki (chocolate) almost had 10 times more votes than Chaka (green onion) during the first week of the presidential election. However, dcinside.com, which is the South Korean version of reddit, heard about this and encouraged everyone to vote for Chaka, because... who doesn't like green onion flavored Chex? right? So during the second week of election, Chaka was in the lead by far.

Shockingly, for arbitrary reasons, Kellogg's invalidated all online votes and transitioned it to an in-person format in which people would vote in amusement parks. Oh, did I mention that Kellogg's gave away freebies such as chocolate and beverages to ONLY THOSE WHO VOTED FOR CHEKI?

Cheki wins

This is very wrong and it is a serious problem hurting our democracy. People were outrageous and mad. Some even started petitions calling for the impeachemnt of 'dictator Cheki'.

People demand for freedom and democracy

Although Kellogg's officially did not acknowledge any election fraud, after 16 years of dictatorship, they finally released the green onion Chex, honoring Chaka and those who voted for him.
